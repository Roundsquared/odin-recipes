Jambalaya

Description

This Cajun dish has origins back to the 1800's where French, Spanish and good ole' backwoods cuisine combined to give us the wonderful dish that is Jambalaya. There are two main variants to this dish, Creole and Cajun. The main difference is in the recipes' use of tomatoes, which wouldn't have been avaliable to the less affluent Cajuns, resulting in a drier Jambalaya.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb. chicken breast
  • Andouille Sausage
  • Yellow Onion
  • Green Bell Pepper
  • Celery
  • Green Onion, for garnish
  • Chicken Broth/Stock
  • Long Grain Rice
  • Bacon
  • Okra
  1. In the bottom of a large pan, cook your bacon (cut into pieces), and your sausage until bacon is done.
  2. Remove sausage and bacon with slotted spoon
  3. Cook Mirepoix in the grease
  4. While your mirepoix is cooking, cook your chicken with whatever seasoning you like.
  5. Cut up your chicken , add okra, chicken, broth, and rice to pot.
  6. Cover and cook until rice has absorbed liquid.
  7. Top with green onion, and enjoy!
